#1 What is statistics, and why can it be useful for cybersecurity?

Statistics is more than just collecting numbers. It is the science of collecting, analyzing, interpreting, presenting, and organizing data. In a world awash with information, statistics provides the tools to make sense of the chaos, uncover hidden patterns, and make informed decisions based on evidence.

It is mainly divided into two branches: descriptive statistics, which summarizes data through indicators like the mean or median and graphs, and inferential statistics, which allows us to draw conclusions about an entire population based on a data sample.

In cybersecurity, this is crucial. Statistical models can detect anomalies in network traffic that might indicate an attack. They can be used for risk analysis, quantifying the probability and impact of potential threats. Furthermore, techniques like classification and regression are fundamental to malware detection and predicting system vulnerabilities. In essence, statistics empowers cybersecurity professionals to move from a reactive to a proactive stance against digital threats.
